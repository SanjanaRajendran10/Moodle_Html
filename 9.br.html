MAC

MacBook Air: Apple's ultra-portable laptop, known for its thin and lightweight design. It typically targets users seeking a balance of portability and performance, suitable for everyday tasks and general computing needs.


MacBook Pro (13-inch and 16-inch): The MacBook Pro lineup includes both 13-inch and 16-inch models. These laptops are known for their powerful performance, high-resolution Retina displays, and advanced features. The 16-inch model, in particular, was renowned for catering to professionals needing higher computational power for tasks such as video editing, programming, and graphic design.


Mac Mini: A compact desktop computer without an integrated monitor, keyboard, or mouse. The Mac Mini is popular for its versatility, serving as an entry-level Mac for users looking to connect it to their own display and peripherals.


Mac Pro: Apple's high-performance desktop workstation designed for professionals and power users requiring extensive computational power. It is a modular computer that allows for extensive customization and expansion.
